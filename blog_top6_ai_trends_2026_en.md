# Top 6 AI Trends That Will Define 2026
## Executive Summary: What Businesses Need to Know Now

*Based on McKinsey, Stanford, and OpenAI research. Original video analysis by Kael (Japanese version available).*

---

## Trend 1: From Build to Buy — The AI Sourcing Pivot

The "build vs. buy" debate for AI capabilities is resolved in 2026. Building in-house costs far more than customizing off-the-shelf solutions. The winning approach: **Buy + Customize**. Evaluate AI agents by ROI, not by whether you built them yourself.

**Action:** If your team is still debating "should we build this ourselves?", run an investment return analysis first. Most cases now favor buying.

---

## Trend 2: Output to Workflow — The Paradigm Shift

The real value isn't answers — it's automating entire workflows. We're moving from **Single-Turn Chat** to **Multi-step Agentic Workflows**. This is the core shift every team needs to understand.

**What this means:** Your AI strategy isn't about getting better answers. It's about identifying recurring processes that can be handed off to AI agents.

**Action:** Pick one recurring deliverable you produce weekly. Map out the steps. Let AI handle the predictable parts while humans stay in the loop for judgment calls.

---

## Trend 3: The End of Data Silos

AI agents break down cross-departmental data barriers. JSON-table thinking is giving way to multi-dimensional analysis. Data that sat in separate silos — marketing, ops, finance — can now be connected by AI agents that traverse organizational boundaries.

**Action:** Identify your most expensive data silo. Ask: "If an AI agent could access this data plus data from 2 other departments, what would change?"

---

## Trend 4: Structuring Tacit Knowledge

Prompt engineering with few-shot examples + context turns tacit knowledge (implicit, undocumented expertise) into structured, repeatable outputs. The automation potential runs into millions of dollars per year for organizations that get this right.

**Action:** Find one expert task your team does that requires specific institutional knowledge. Build a few-shot prompt that captures that knowledge. Test whether AI reproduces the pattern.

---

## Trend 5: From Benchmarking to Structured Evals

Most companies outside the top AI labs lack proper evaluation systems. "How good is our AI?" is becoming as important as "How good is our AI strategy?". The shift: **Measurement-Driven AI Development**.

**Action:** If you don't have a framework for evaluating AI performance, build one now. It's the foundation for every other investment decision.

---

## Trend 6: Search to Execute — The Agent Era Begins

The biggest shift: AI is moving from **"search for me"** to **"execute this for me"**. This is the real meaning of "Agent Year" — not chatbots that answer questions, but AI that takes action on your behalf.

**Why it matters:** Google's Gemini OS-level integration and similar moves represent a fundamental change in what AI means. It's no longer a tool you query. It's a proxy that acts.

**Action:** Start experimenting with AI agents that take real actions (draft emails, update records, schedule tasks) rather than just finding information.

---

## Key Takeaways for 2026

1. **Agentic Workflow** is the core competitive advantage — not better chatbots
2. **Build vs. Buy** decisions should be driven by Evals, not intuition
3. **Data silos** represent low-hanging fruit for AI agents to unlock
4. **Tacit knowledge structuring** via few-shot prompting is immediately actionable
5. **Search → Execute** shift will redefine what "having an AI strategy" means
6. **Measurement** (Evals) becomes the foundation of all AI investment
